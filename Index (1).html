The Ancient Beginnings of Coffee

Coffee's journey through history is as rich and complex as its flavor. From its legendary discovery to its global spread, coffee has shaped cultures and economies for centuries.

The traditional Ethiopian coffee ceremony, a ritual that dates back centuries

Coffee beans being sorted in Ethiopia, the birthplace of coffee

The Journey Through Time

9th Century

Legend of Kaldi, the Ethiopian goatherd who discovered coffee when his goats became energetic after eating coffee berries.

15th Century

Coffee cultivation and trade begins in Yemen, where Sufi monks used it to stay awake during night prayers.

17th Century

Coffee reaches Europe through Venetian traders, with the first coffeehouse opening in Venice in 1645.

18th Century

Coffee plants are smuggled out of the Arab world, leading to cultivation in Java, the Caribbean, and Latin America.

20th Century

Instant coffee is invented, revolutionizing coffee consumption worldwide.

21st Century

Third wave coffee movement emphasizes quality, sustainability, and direct trade relationships.

Our Commitment to Quality

From Bean to Cup

At Brewed Traditions, we source only the finest specialty grade coffee beans (graded 80+ points) from ethical growers. Each batch is roasted in small quantities to bring out the optimal flavor characteristics of the bean's origin. Our roasting process is both an art and a science, carefully monitoring temperature and time to highlight each bean's unique qualities.

We maintain direct relationships with coffee farmers across Ethiopia, Colombia, Brazil, and Guatemala, ensuring fair prices and sustainable farming practices. This direct trade approach means we pay 25-30% above fair trade prices, investing in long-term partnerships that benefit both farmers and coffee lovers.

Brewing Methods We Love

Different brewing methods highlight different aspects of coffee's complex flavor profile. Here are our favorite ways to brew:

Pour Over (V60)

Produces a clean, bright cup that highlights delicate floral and fruity notes. Ideal for light to medium roasts. Brew time: 2-3 minutes.

French Press

Creates a full-bodied coffee with rich oils and intense flavor. Perfect for bold, dark roasts. Brew time: 4 minutes.

AeroPress

Versatile method that can produce espresso-like shots or clean filtered coffee. Quick and portable. Brew time: 1-2 minutes.
